Manufacturer API
1
Plug in your OBD-II dongle
The OBD-II port is under your dashboard on the driver's side, within 60cm of the steering wheel. It looks like a small trapezoid socket. Plug the dongle in — it powers on automatically when your ignition is on.
2
Scan for Bluetooth devices
Enable Bluetooth on your phone or laptop. Click Scan below — we'll detect all ELM327-compatible adapters nearby. Most dongles show as "OBDII", "ELM327", or "V-Link".
Detected Devices
Click Scan to find OBD adapters
3
Read live data from your car
Once paired, click Read Vehicle Data. We'll pull engine data, fault codes, fuel level, battery voltage, and GPS coordinates directly from your ECU.
4
Register adapter with your vehicle
Select the vehicle this adapter belongs to. The adapter serial number is stored against your VIN. Every hourly scan is automatically pushed to your DCP profile and health dashboard.
5
Hourly sync active
Your vehicle now sends health data to The Automat Hub every hour. You'll receive alerts when new fault codes appear. The DCP is updated after every inspection.
